Platform

Infrastructure for the difficult middle layer

Akreon’s platform direction is centered on the layer between research outputs and deployed system behavior.

The hard part is rarely just the algorithm or just the deployment target. It is the interface between them: how methods are represented, constrained, validated, integrated, monitored, and adapted for use in real systems.

We are building toward infrastructure that helps teams move from promising technical capability to controlled deployment in environments where latency, uncertainty, compute limits, safety requirements, and system interactions all matter.

The emphasis is on what the platform enables: cleaner research-to-engineering handoff, more disciplined system integration, and a more credible path from prototype behavior to fielded capability.

Representative layers

Method integration

Connecting models, planning, control, estimation, and learned components to production-facing system boundaries.

Validation and safeguards

Structuring tests, operational limits, and evidence artifacts that support safer deployment decisions.

Deployment architecture

Framing runtime, interfaces, and system decomposition so performance survives real-world conditions.

Selective technical depth

Depending on the problem, this layer can involve dynamics, controls, optimization, planning, estimation, sensor fusion, embedded systems, autonomy architecture, ROS2-based integration, sensing workflows, or related system engineering concerns. These are examples of depth around the platform story, not the platform story itself.
